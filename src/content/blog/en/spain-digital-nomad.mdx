# Spain Digital Nomad Visa 2026: The Complete Guide

The Digital Nomad Visa is a Spanish residence permit (ВНЖ) designed for individuals who work remotely for foreign companies and clients. <Highlight>The residence card is issued immediately for three years</Highlight>, requiring no real estate investment or setting up a local business.

We have been working with this program since its launch and consistently see the same pattern: applications are almost never rejected because the applicant lacks sufficient income. <Highlight>Rejections happen because of paperwork</Highlight>. A health insurance policy with a deductible (co-payment), a contract that fails to mention remote work, or a background check certificate formatted incorrectly—and the file is sent back for revision.

This guide covers the current 2026 requirements, realistic timelines, and the critical nuances applicants usually discover only after receiving a rejection.
In short: <Highlight>income from €2,849/month</Highlight>, a 3-year card, a processing time of 20 working days, and your family can apply together with you.

The program is designed for individuals who earn their income from outside of Spain and can work from anywhere.

## Income Requirements: How Much Do You Need to Earn in 2026?

The income threshold for the Spanish residence permit is not fixed—it is tied to the SMI (Salario Mínimo Interprofesional), the country's minimum wage. When the SMI changes, the applicant requirements adjust automatically.

In 2026, the main applicant must prove an income of 200% of the SMI—which equals <Highlight>€2,849 per month or €34,188 per year</Highlight>.

There are two major pitfalls where applicants frequently stumble.

First: <Highlight>spousal incomes cannot be combined</Highlight>. Spanish authorities look solely at the income of the main applicant—relying on a joint family budget will not work.

Second: currency fluctuations. Income can be earned in dollars, pounds, dirhams, etc.—but upon submission, it is converted into euros using the ECB exchange rate on the application date. Therefore, we always recommend having a <Highlight>5–10% buffer above the minimum threshold</Highlight>: the exchange rate could shift right before submission, leaving an applicant who was "just hitting the mark" suddenly short of the requirement.

## Required Documents for the Spanish Residence Permit

Approval depends not on the volume of papers, but on how well they align with one another. The income shown in bank statements must match the contract, and the employer's letter must not contradict the service agreement. Any discrepancy triggers an additional document request (дозапрос) and delays the process.

### International Passport

Copies of all pages, including blank ones. This is a standard requirement of the UGE-CE.

### Contract with Employer or Clients

The cornerstone document. The professional relationship with the employer or client must have existed for at least three months prior to application. Ideally, the contract should specify:

<BlogList
	text={[
		"Job title or the nature of services provided",
		"Start date and duration of the contract",
		"Compensation amount",
		"Explicit permission to work remotely from Spain",
	]}
/>

The most frequent issue here: <Highlight>the contract lacks a direct statement regarding remote work</Highlight>. Technically, this isn't always a violation, but in such cases, we draft a separate employer letter to address the issue beforehand.

### Company Documents

Spanish authorities vet the employer as well as the applicant. You must prove the company has been active for at least one year: for Russian legal entities, an Egryul/Egrip extract with an apostille is required; for foreign entities, the official registration documents from the country of incorporation.

### Employer Letter

This confirms your right to work remotely from Spain and mirrors the terms of cooperation. Many view this as a formality—in reality, this letter resolves any ambiguities when the contract is not detailed enough.

### Bank Statements

For the last three months, showing regular deposits that match the declared income. Business owners/freelancers must additionally provide invoices, while W2-equivalent employees must provide payslips.

### Criminal Record Certificate (Background Check)

Requires an apostille and a sworn translation (traducción jurada) into Spanish, alongside a declaration of no criminal record in your previous countries of residence.

<Highlight>Do not order this certificate at the last minute</Highlight>. While the rest of your paperwork is being prepared, its validity window shrinks—and applicants often find themselves needing a new one by the time they submit.

## Health Insurance — Where Most Rejections Happen

The policy must be issued by a Spanish insurance company and provide coverage equivalent to the national healthcare system.

However, the main pitfall lies elsewhere. The policy <Highlight>must not include a deductible or co-payment (co-pago)</Highlight>. Even a minimal co-payment makes the insurance non-compliant.

This is the most common and frustrating reason for rejection: an applicant meets the income, contract, and experience criteria—only to be turned down over a "cheaper" insurance policy.

### Proof of Legal Entry

If applying from within Spain: a Schengen entry stamp, boarding passes, or an entry declaration (declaración de entrada) if you entered via another Schengen country.

### MIT Form and Government Fee

The final step. The receipt showing payment of the processing fee must be included in the application package.

## How Long Does the Processing Take?

The official processing time at the UGE-CE is <Highlight>20 working days</Highlight> from the date the application is registered. In practice, the entire journey from the initial consultation to getting the physical card takes about two months. Most of this time is spent preparing documents: apostilles, translations, and certificates depend on multiple government agencies at once.

### Step 1. Document Audit

Before submission, we ensure the documents do not contradict one another. This is when details unnoticed by the applicant usually surface: the income listed in the contract does not match the actual bank deposits, or the employer's letter conflicts with the contract. Fixing this beforehand is significantly easier than responding to an official request for information.

### Step 2. Package Preparation

Apostilles, legalization, and sworn translations. This is typically the longest stage.

### Step 3. Arrival in Spain

Most of our clients submit their applications while legally residing in the country on a tourist status. This allows them to request residency directly, bypassing the need for a consular visa.

### Step 4. Submission to the UGE-CE

Documents are submitted electronically to the Unidad de Grandes Empresas y Colectivos Estratégicos. If the agency requires clarification, they will issue an additional document request with a specific deadline. <Highlight>An additional document request is not a rejection</Highlight>. It is a standard procedural step, especially for complex cases.

### Step 5. TIE Residence Card

Following a favorable resolution, an appointment is booked for fingerprinting, followed by the collection of the physical TIE card (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero). The TIE is your official proof of residency.

## Residence Permit Validity and Renewal

The initial residence permit is issued <Highlight>straight away for three years</Highlight>—this is a key advantage over most European alternatives, which usually start with a one-year permit.

This is followed by a two-year renewal, totaling five years of legal residency under the program, which opens the path to long-term residency.

Upon renewal, you will need to re-verify the same type of income. If your remote contract has turned into local Spanish employment, or if your share of Spanish clients has grown beyond 20%, the renewal could fail on those grounds.

## Taxes on the Digital Nomad Visa

Residents under this program can apply for a special tax regime—commonly known as the <Highlight>Beckham Law</Highlight>. It allows expats to pay a flat tax rate on Spanish-sourced income instead of progressive tax brackets during the initial years.

However, there is a counter that runs independently of your visa type: <Highlight>183 days</Highlight>. Spending more than 183 days in Spain during a calendar year makes you a tax resident—meaning your global income becomes subject to Spanish taxation.

We calculate the potential tax burden before submitting documents, not after you receive your card. This is a crucial step to avoid an unpleasant surprise a year down the road when it is too late to make changes.

And a major benefit for the household budget: after obtaining residency, <Highlight>family members have the right to work locally in Spain</Highlight> without needing a separate work permit.

## The Path to Permanent Residency and Citizenship

After five years of lawful residence, you become eligible to apply for long-term resident status. However, what matters is your actual physical presence rather than just the time on paper—extended periods spent outside the country can reset your progress.

Citizenship is accessible after a minimum of ten years, requiring proof of cultural integration and passing language/culture exams.

If Spain is being considered as a long-term home, it is wise to map out a comprehensive immigration strategy from day one, rather than focusing solely on the duration of the first card.

## Why Applications Get Rejected: 5 Real Reasons

It is almost never due to failing to meet the core criteria. It is almost always due to the paperwork.

1. <Highlight>Insurance with a co-payment (co-pago)</Highlight> — Reason #1
2. The employer's letter contradicts the employment contract
3. The income deposited in the bank statements does not match the figure in the contract
4. The criminal record certificate lacks an apostille or features an incorrect translation
5. Document translations were not performed by a certified sworn translator (traductor jurado)

On its own, each error might seem like a minor detail. However, during review, it is the collective accuracy of these details that shapes the decision on your file.
